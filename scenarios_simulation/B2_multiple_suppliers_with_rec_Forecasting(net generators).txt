\subsection{Scenario B2-Forecasts: Multiple Suppliers with REC -- REC-Aware Forecasting (Net Generators)}
\label{sec:results_B2fn}

This section presents the results of Scenario B2 with REC-aware forecasting under the net generator configuration. Supplier~A (BG\_A) serves all consumers, while Supplier~B (BG\_B) represents a portfolio consisting exclusively of prosumers operating as net generators. Market schedules incorporate expected REC energy exchanges ex-ante, so the scheduled balancing group positions reflect the residual demand or surplus after internal community transfers.

\subsubsection{Balancing Performance}

Table~\ref{tab:B2fn_balancing_combined} summarises the annual balancing group outcomes for both suppliers. Forecast errors introduce substantial imbalance volumes: Supplier~A's scheduled position of 148.33~MWh overshoots its actual net position of 105.09~MWh, producing a long imbalance of $-$43.23~MWh and incurring \EUR{1,472.87} in penalties. Conversely, Supplier~B's forecast underestimates generation, resulting in a short imbalance of +43.24~MWh that yields \EUR{1,465.33} in balancing rewards. The imbalances are primarily attributable to forecast uncertainty in RES generation schedules.

\subsubsection{Cost Structure}

Figure~\ref{fig:costs_B2fn} presents the monthly cost components of both suppliers. Supplier~A primarily incurs wholesale procurement costs to meet consumer demand. In contrast, Supplier~B’s costs are dominated by feed-in compensation payments to prosumers for electricity not absorbed within the REC. Because Supplier~B’s portfolio contains only net generators, it does not incur demand-side procurement costs. Balancing-related expenditures remain minor for both suppliers.

\subsubsection{Revenue Structure}

Figure~\ref{fig:revenue_components_B2fn} illustrates the revenue composition of both suppliers. Supplier~A’s revenue is largely derived from retail electricity sales to consumers. Supplier~B, by contrast, generates revenue mainly from wholesale market sales of surplus electricity and balancing rewards. Retail billing revenue is absent for Supplier~B because its portfolio consists entirely of net-generating prosumers.

\subsubsection{Overall Financial Outcome}

Table~\ref{tab:B2fn_profit_combined} summarises the annual financial outcomes for both suppliers. Supplier~A records a net profit of \EUR{15,107.73}, driven by retail electricity sales of \EUR{21,123.93} which comfortably offset market purchase costs of \EUR{4,749.35} and balancing penalties of \EUR{1,472.87}. Supplier~B achieves a net profit of \EUR{1,728.87}, with wholesale market revenue of \EUR{1,568.91} and balancing rewards of \EUR{1,465.33} exceeding its feed-in compensation costs of \EUR{1,107.46} and balancing penalties of \EUR{197.91}. Both suppliers are profitable in this configuration, although Supplier~A's margin is substantially larger due to the retail markup on consumer sales.
